# Ideate

**Multi-cycle evolutionary idea generation that pressure-tests ideas before presenting them.**

BeCreative is for quick divergent ideation. Ideate is for when you need ideas that have been through something — cross-pollinated, stress-tested, selected against competing alternatives. The output is a smaller set of stronger ideas, not a long list of possibilities.

## The 9 Phases

1. **Seed** — generate N initial ideas without filtering. Quantity before quality.
2. **Diverge** — take each seed in a radically different direction. Mutation.
3. **Cross-pollinate** — combine elements from different seeds to create hybrids.
4. **Stress-test** — what would kill each surviving idea? Apply adversarial pressure.
5. **Prune** — eliminate ideas that don't survive stress. No rescue.
6. **Refine** — strengthen the survivors. Fix what the stress-test exposed.
7. **Select** — rank by: novelty, feasibility, fit to the stated goal.
8. **Synthesize** — can elements from different refined ideas be combined into a stronger whole?
9. **Present** — output the top 1–3 with rationale for why they survived.

## How It Differs From BeCreative

| Dimension | BeCreative | Ideate |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| Cycles | 1 (Verbalized Sampling) | 9 phases |
| Pressure testing | No | Yes (Phase 4) |
| Output | Single best option | 1–3 survivors with rationale |
| Time budget | E2+ | E4+ |
| When | Quick divergent thinking | Deep evolutionary refinement |

## Use When

- BeCreative produced options but none feel right
- The problem is large and poorly bounded (many valid directions)
- You need ideas that have survived adversarial scrutiny
- Multi-cycle refinement is worth the time investment
- The final idea will be consequential enough to justify the depth

## Gotchas

- 9 phases is not 9 turns. Phases can be rapid if the problem is well-bounded.
- Pruning is hard. The instinct is to rescue ideas. Don't. Ideas that don't survive stress-testing are not ready.
- Cross-pollination (Phase 3) is where the most unexpected ideas emerge. Don't skip it.
- The output should be 1–3 ideas, not a list of 10. Selection discipline is the skill.
